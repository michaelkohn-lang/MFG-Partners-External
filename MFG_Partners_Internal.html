1. Deal Sourcing

Objective: Build and maintain a proprietary pipeline of middle-market sports service businesses

Checklist

  • Map target verticals: travel, sponsorship, insurance, financial ops
  • Identify potential targets through ownership group networks and league contacts
  • Monitor industry publications, conference attendee lists, and transaction databases
  • Leverage Jordan's sports ownership network for warm introductions
  • Track inbound referrals from existing portfolio company operators
  • Maintain CRM pipeline with contact status, last touchpoint, and next action
  • Score opportunities against platform fit criteria (revenue model, client base, margin profile)
  • Generate weekly sourcing report for team review

2. Screening

Objective: Evaluate target fit against MFG's acquisition criteria before committing diligence resources

Checklist

  • Confirm contracted/recurring revenue model (retainers, commissions, long-term agreements)
  • Verify client base includes professional teams, leagues, or ownership groups
  • Assess margin profile: target >50% gross margin, asset-light model
  • Evaluate cross-sell potential with existing/planned portfolio companies
  • Identify key person risk and operator dependency
  • Review competitive landscape within the specific vertical
  • Assess founder/operator willingness to transact and integration readiness
  • Produce screening memo with go/no-go recommendation

3. Due Diligence

Objective: Comprehensive evaluation of financial, commercial, legal, and operational risk before commitment

Checklist

  • Request and organize data room (3 years financials, contracts, org chart, insurance, legal)
  • Financial DD: quality of earnings, revenue sustainability, working capital analysis
  • Commercial DD: client concentration, contract renewal rates, competitive positioning
  • Legal DD: entity structure, outstanding litigation, IP ownership, regulatory compliance
  • Operational DD: systems, processes, key personnel, capacity constraints
  • Management assessment: interviews with founder/operator and key team members
  • Red flag review and risk matrix development
  • Produce DD summary report with findings and risk-adjusted recommendation

4. Valuation

Objective: Determine fair value range using multiple methodologies with sports-specific overlays

Checklist

  • Build comparable company analysis (public comps + private transaction comps)
  • Develop precedent transactions analysis from sports services M&A
  • Construct DCF model with base, upside, and downside scenarios
  • Apply sports-specific valuation adjustments (client concentration, contract duration, cross-sell premium)
  • Determine implied multiple ranges (EV/Revenue, EV/EBITDA)
  • Build football field / valuation summary chart
  • Stress-test key assumptions and document sensitivity analysis
  • Present valuation range with recommended offer price and justification

5. Negotiation

Objective: Optimize terms while maintaining counterparty alignment and relationship integrity

Checklist

  • Define BATNA (best alternative to negotiated agreement) and walk-away price
  • Map counterparty motivations, constraints, and decision-making authority
  • Develop negotiation strategy memo with concession plan and red lines
  • Prepare term sheet with headline economics and governance provisions
  • Conduct counterparty meetings with prepared talking points
  • Track all term modifications and counterproposals in negotiation log
  • Identify creative structuring options to bridge valuation gaps
  • Secure verbal agreement on key terms before moving to documentation

6. Deal Structuring

Objective: Design transaction economics, governance, and entity structure that align incentives

Checklist

  • Determine acquisition structure (asset purchase, equity purchase, merger, partnership)
  • Design ownership split and governance rights (board seats, veto rights, approval thresholds)
  • Structure earnout/holdback provisions tied to performance milestones
  • Define management incentive alignment (equity rollover, performance bonuses)
  • Model cap table and waterfall economics across scenarios
  • Draft partnership/operating agreement key terms
  • Coordinate with outside counsel on entity formation and legal documentation
  • Produce deal structure summary for team and advisor review

7. Execution & Close

Objective: Execute definitive agreements, secure financing, and complete legal close

Checklist

  • Finalize and execute definitive purchase/partnership agreement
  • Complete entity formation (LLC, corporate filings, EIN, bank accounts)
  • Secure bank financing or capital deployment authorization
  • Satisfy closing conditions (regulatory approvals, third-party consents, rep & warranty confirmations)
  • Coordinate wire transfers and funds flow
  • Execute ancillary agreements (employment, non-compete, transition services)
  • Obtain D&O and commercial insurance coverage
  • File all required governmental and regulatory notices
  • Distribute closing binder to all parties

8. Integration (100-Day Plan)

Objective: Execute systematic integration to capture platform synergies and establish operating rhythm

Checklist

  • Day 1: Announce transaction to employees, clients, and partners
  • Days 1–14: Onboard operator to MFG systems (reporting, communication, finance)
  • Days 1–30: Establish reporting cadence (weekly ops call, monthly financial reporting)
  • Days 1–30: Map existing client base and identify cross-sell opportunities
  • Days 30–60: Execute first cross-sell introductions across portfolio
  • Days 30–60: Implement shared financial infrastructure (chart of accounts, AP/AR processes)
  • Days 60–90: Evaluate operator performance against integration milestones
  • Day 90: Produce 90-day integration review with performance scorecard
  • Day 100: Transition from integration mode to ongoing operations

9. Ongoing Operations

Objective: Maximize portfolio company value through operational excellence, cross-sell, and strategic support

Checklist

  • Monthly financial reporting: P&L, balance sheet, cash flow, KPI dashboard
  • Weekly operator check-in calls (15 min standing meeting)
  • Quarterly portfolio review with full team (performance, risks, opportunities)
  • Track cross-sell revenue attribution across portfolio companies
  • Monitor client satisfaction and contract renewal pipeline
  • Identify and execute add-on acquisition or expansion opportunities
  • Produce quarterly investor update with portfolio performance summary
  • Annual budget planning and strategic goal-setting with each operator
  • Maintain institutional documentation for all operating decisions
